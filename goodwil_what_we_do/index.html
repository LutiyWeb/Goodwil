Our investment priority - SPAC

SPAC is a special purpose acquisition company that replaces traditional IPOs and accelerates the listing process, reducing risks and increasing profits.

To run SPAC, the founding team needs to:

01
Prepare

Prepare legal and organizational structures

02
Pay

Make a proactive payment

03
Recieve

Recieve funds from an investment bank

After forming the founding team, defining the SPAC goals and resolving legal nuances, the organizers move on to the final stage of launching the company.

At this stage, SPAC is raising the necessary funds for the subsequent takeover / merger with a company wishing to go public.

Financing is carried out with the participation of an investment bank (underwriter), which allocates the necessary funds after the team has made a proactive payment. This payment is calculated based on the total amount of attracted investments and amounts to 3.5-7% of the total investment.

If the founding team does not have enough funds for a proactive payment, Goodwill Capital joins the process as a Sponsor. The fund makes a proactive payment in exchange for 11% of the total SPAC shares.

The shares are offered at a significant discount due to the fact that this process occurs before the SPAC is listed.

Upon completion of all registration procedures and the transfer of the required amount by the investment bank, SPAC shares are listed on the stock exchange, getting into free sale. At this point, the cost of each automatically increases to $10.

The cost is determined by the rules of the SEC (US Securities and Exchange Commission) and is backed by the funds actually reserved in the company's trust account.

During the life of the SPAC, the value of the stock will likely increase. This may be due to positive news, such as: the announcement of the company takeover / merger, the acquisition / merger procedure itself, the subsequent formation of a public company.

Goodwill Capital does not set the goal of long-term investing in shares of a public company and fully playing on the course of their value.

The task of the fund is to sell shares at the optimal moment after the lifting of the moratorium (1 year from the date of SPAC creation).
